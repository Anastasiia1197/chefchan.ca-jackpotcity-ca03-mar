Healthy Habits and Strategies for Enjoyable Entertainment

Responsible gambling begins with awareness and proper preparation. Set limits in advance for both time and money you wish to spend on online entertainment. Always treat games as a form of entertainment, never as a way to earn money or recoup losses. Plan regular breaks during sessions and maintain balance with other hobbies and responsibilities in your life across Canada. These are the fundamental principles of healthy gaming habits.

Warning Signs of Problematic Gaming Behaviour

Early recognition of warning signs is crucial for maintaining control. Pay attention to signs such as: difficulty stopping play, feeling the need to gamble more to achieve the same excitement, lying to friends or family about time or money spent on gambling, financial problems or borrowing money to gamble, and neglecting work, studies, or social contacts. Do you recognise one or more of these signs in yourself or someone else? It's time to take action.

Debunking Common Myths About Gambling

There are persistent myths about gambling that can fuel risky behaviours. It is important to know them:

  • Online gambling is not an investment: The platform always has a statistical advantage in the long term. Gambling to make money is a dangerous mindset.
  • No "sure" strategy exists: No tactic or system can overcome the randomness (RNG) of fair slot games.
  • "Chasing losses" thinking: Losses cannot be recouped by playing longer or more; each round is independent. This concept is known as the "gambler's fallacy".

Support for Loved Ones and Families

Problem gambling has a significant impact not only on the gambler but also on their immediate surroundings. As a family member, partner, or friend, it is important to recognise the signs and know how to approach such a person. Open, non-judgmental communication is the first step. There are also specific support groups and helplines for loved ones across Canada.

Professional Support Organisations in Canada

If you notice that gambling is becoming a problem, seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness. In Canada 🇨🇦, there are several trustworthy organisations that offer free and anonymous help. Provincial regulatory bodies provide information and resources. For direct help and advice, you can turn to specialised institutions.

Protection of Minors and Prevention

By law, regulated platforms in Canada are required to implement rigorous age verification (19+ in most provinces, 18+ in Alberta, Manitoba, and Quebec). Examples of Self-Restriction Tools (on Platforms)

  • Daily, weekly, and monthly deposit limits
  • Time limits per session or per day
  • Self-exclusion for periods from 24 hours to permanent (e.g., via provincial self-exclusion programs like PlaySmart in Ontario)
  • Reality checks (notifications about playing time and spending)
  • Setting loss or win limits
  • Ability to set a break from play for a selected period

Important Support Organisations in Canada

  • Centre for Addiction and Mental Health (CAMH): Leading Canadian resource for addiction research and support.
  • ConnexOntario: Free and confidential health services information for mental health, addictions, and problem gambling in Ontario. Call 1-866-531-2600
  • Problem Gambling Help Line (across Canada): 1-888-795-6111 (available 24/7).
  • Gamblers Anonymous (GA) Canada: Organises support groups following the 12-step programme across Canadian cities.
  • Responsible Gambling Council (RGC): Provides information, advice and support for anyone affected by gambling-related harms.
  • British Columbia Responsible & Problem Gambling Program: 1-888-795-6111 (24/7 helpline).
  • Alberta Health Services – Addiction & Mental Health: Provides confidential support and treatment referrals.
  • iGaming Ontario (iGO): Regulates online gaming in Ontario and provides responsible gambling resources.
